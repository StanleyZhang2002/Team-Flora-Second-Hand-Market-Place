## Process - Reflection


#### Q1. Decisions that turned out well

List **process-related** (i.e. team organization and how you work) decisions that, in retrospect, turned out to be successful.

- After the third week, we do the pair coding instead of coding by oneself.

reason: At first few week of the coding, everyone working on their own part and commit to their own branch. This leads to a huge problem when someone is busy since we cannot reach them immediately so we cannot fix the problem and understand the code each other write. This problem makes first few weeks of coding really drag. So after we discussed in our regular weekly meeting, we decides to pair coding so that if someone is busy, one member in one part can explain and fix the code while other part of code has some problem and this turns out really successful for us where if our frontend need something changed in the backend, as long as one member from the backend is free, then this problem can be fixed.
  
- We did face to face coding instead of the online coding.

reason: Most people are busy and not manage time well. When we do online coding, most people will say “i will do it later” “that’s is easy, i will do it tomorrow” and the works are set asides. After a few week of no progress.  We manage to have at least one times a week to do the face to face coding and meeting in-person to focus on the project instead of online. Since if we have online meetings, we may not be as focused but if we are in-person, we can focus more on what we work on and we can also motivate each other to do the work right now during our meeting time. This really helps us speed up our progress in coding and producing the final product.

- We use the mongodb instead of python related database.

reason: Most of our team members don’t have any experience working with database. One of our member suggest to use mongodb since it is easy to learn and he volunteer that he can manage and maintain the database code. So instead of the python own database, we used the mongodb and as a result, our frontend and backend programmer does not worry too much on database part of the program and our database is managed really well. Another benefits that this decision brings us is that we do not need to worry about setting up the database locally but instead, just have teh database on cloud.


#### Q2. Decisions that did not turn out as well as we hoped

List **process-related** (i.e. team organization and how you work) decisions that, in retrospect, were not as successful as you thought they would be.


- Leaving the work of combining frontend and backend and deploying the application to the last minute.

reason: At beginning, everyone was confident with the progress thinking that the code are not going to take long time so at the beginning, we didn't manage our progress or workflow too well. As the deadline close, someone suddenly quit the project because of workload in other courses. But we still decided to keep our regular amount of feature for deliverable 2 because we are still confident that we can finish frontend and backend on time. But as a result, we way underestimate the complexity of combining frontend and backend together and deploying the application which causes us a lot of trouble and making us behind on the progress. We encounter lots of difficulties when combining front and back end such as type disagreement when passing data between frontend and backend or name mismatch. Deployment also gives us trouble where we cannot figure out how to use proxy in Heroku so for future deliverables, we will try to work on it as soon as possible rather than leaving it close to the deadline to minimize the effect of emergency situations. 

- Use weekly regular meeting not to explain the progress of each part of our project, but use it as a time for purely group coding.

reason: Within our team, we divide up tasks where students has part of the project that they manage leaving some students working at the edge of the project such as working on testing codes or working on managing database so that they do not have strong enough understanding of how specifically our project work. In our regular meeting, we decide to just use it as a time where we code together as a group so students working on database does not have a chance to know what stage our project is in causing them not having a close understanding of features and UIs of our project. We realize this problem when we do a demoing of features within our own group during the review meeting and make them caught up to be on the same page. Furthermore, we decided to spend roughly 5 minutes in each regular weekly meeting to update everyone on current features to avoid this situation.
